My professional life can be segmented into three phases: the military, food and beverage operations/operations, and currently logistics supervision with a recent side job in edtech. I served in the Air Force in the 1990's as an avionics technician, working on F-15's and F-111's. I left after my enlistment ended to go to college full time. After taking a job in food and beverage operations to supplement my GI Bill funding, my focus shifted away from college. I took several roles before landing with Valencia Corporation in 2004. I remained employed with Valencia until 2018, ultimately rising to the role of food and beverage director.

When I left Valencia in 2018, it was out of a combination of three factors: burnout, a desire to find more intellectually satisfying work, and finally to deal with personal issues. In this period, I sought out a job which could meet my basic financial needs while allowing me a surplus of time to reorient  myself in the world. This is how I ended up working in logistics at FedEx. I initially did not take a role in supervision but eventually relented for financial reasons.

While I had tinkered in the 1980's and 1990's with tech, I didn't develop a serious interest until 2021. With more time to explore ideas during this period, I delved into learning how to program. I took a rather circuitous path, and, after working through the Learn Enough website, found Launch School. I worked through the prep courses and the Introduction to Ruby course but did not move on. I fully intended to but life got very busy in 2024.

After receiving supplemental education funding from FedEx in 2023, I enrolled in the University of Memphis Global to finally finish my Bachelors degree. Before I began, in January 2024, I had an unexpected medical issue(heart surgery) that required some focus on recovery. Simultaneously, my father passed suddenly, which also required quite a bit of attention. While all this was going on, I was offered a job with Podium Education in 2025 after finishing their Coding for Data with Python course. I accepted the position to bolster my finances as well as to get some experience in tech. My position was as a team lead, assisting in zoom coding seminars and grading student submissions and portfolio projects. I worked for Podium all through 2025 and greatly enjoyed helping students while working with my fellow team leads and the instructors.

2024 and 2025 were busy. I graduated in December 2025 with a Bachelors degree in Professional Studies with a concentration in Information Technology. At that time,after some reflection, I decide to concentrate my focus on improving my programming skills. To enable this, I kept FedEx as my only employment and started in late January putting 15 to 20 hours a week studying python. I recently finished the Introductory courses and have been drilling ever since.

My intent going forward is to work through the Launch School Core Curriculum to deepen my Python competency. This is for two practical reasons. The first is to provide me with the skills I intend to use in data modeling in 2027 in my pursuit of a Master's degree in Regenerative Biology at the University of Texas at San Antonio. The second is to provide me with options for earning extra income by freelancing or possibly transitioning to a development role at FedEx while I finish my master's.
